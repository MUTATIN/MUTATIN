Lab safety 1

Lab safety includes practices to prevent accidents and exposure to hazardous chemicals and materials. Personal protective equipment (PPE) Wear the appropriate PPE, such as gloves, lab coats, goggles, and face shields Remove PPE before leaving the lab Remove gloves before handling common items Hygiene

Lab safety 2

Wash hands and arms with soap and water after removing gloves Avoid eating, drinking, or applying cosmetics in the lab Waste disposal Use designated containers for chemical waste Don't pour chemicals down the sink Laboratory practices

Lab safety 3

Keep work areas clean and uncluttered Don't block emergency showers, eye washes, exits, or hallways Label your work space and containers Participate in safety exercises Chemical handling Avoid direct contact with chemicals Don't smell, inhale, or taste chemicals Use proper storage containers Equipment

Lab safety 4

Understand how to safely operate equipment Use fume hoods to capture hazardous gases, fumes, and vapors Use biosafety cabinets to protect against biological agents Laboratory safety rules Follow basic safety rules, Understand the hazards of materials you are working with, and Work to reduce potential risks
